\section*{RESEARCH INTERESTS}

\noindent
I work in extremal combinatorics and graph theory. My current interests are in spectral extremal graph theory, intersection graphs of paths, intersecting set systems, graph convexity, and majority coloring games. I have also worked on colorings and list colorings of graphs embedded in surfaces, and on the ranks of structured matrices arising from tournaments.

\subsection*{\textsc{Current Projects}}

\noindent
\textbf{Majority coloring game on trees} \\
With Saraswati Girish Nanoti (IISc Bangalore).
Investigating optimal strategies and computational complexity for the two-player majority coloring game, particularly on trees.

\noindent
\textbf{\(\triangle\)-convexity in graphs} \\
Investigating convexity structures in graphs, particularly computing dimensional parameters such as the exchange number and Carath\'eodory number for \(\triangle\)-convexity, and characterizing the extremal examples.

\noindent
\textbf{Brualdi--Hoffman-type extremal problems} \\
With M.\ Rajesh Kannan (IIT Hyderabad). Investigating spectral extremal problems for \(F\)-free graphs on \(m\) edges, extending recent results for the case \(F = K_3\) to \(F = K_4\) and beyond.

\noindent
\textbf{Intersection graphs of paths} \\
With Niranjan Balachandran (IIT Bombay), Shagnik Das (National Taiwan University), Umesh Shankar (IISc Bangalore). Investigating the path-representation number \(\mathsf{prn}(G)\) of a graph \(G\): bounds in terms of other graph parameters, algorithms for computing optimal path representations, computational complexity of the parameter for different graph classes.

\noindent
\textbf{Fractional intersecting families} \\
Continuing investigation of the Balachandran--Mathew--Mishra conjecture on the maximum size of fractional \(\theta\)-intersecting families over \([n]\), building on \href{https://doi.org/10.37236/12900}{Balachandran--Das--Sankarnarayanan (2025)} and \href{https://doi.org/10.37236/11651}{Balachandran--Bhattacharya--Kher--Mathew--Sankarnarayanan (2023)}.
