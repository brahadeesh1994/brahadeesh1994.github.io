The Combinatorial Applications & Techniques (CAT) Lab

The CAT Lab is a research group at the Department of Mathematics, IIT Jodhpur, focused on extremal problems in combinatorics and graph theory, led by Brahadeesh Sankarnarayanan. Broadly, in extremal combinatorics one asks what the largest or smallest discrete structures are with respect to natural combinatorial constraints. One is also often interested in whether such extremal objects are unique, and whether objects that are close to these extremal ones have any structural constraints.

Work in the lab currently runs along five threads. In the majority coloring game, two players alternately color the vertices of a tree while keeping every vertex in the minority of its own neighborhood, and the lab studies the resulting majority game chromatic number for binary and ternary trees. In \(\triangle\)-convexity, the lab bounds the Carathéodory number of a graph in terms of its number of triangles, by a component-counting argument that is expected to extend to exchange numbers. In Brualdi–Hoffman-type extremal problems, the question is how large the spectral radius of a \(K_4\)-free graph on \(m\) edges can be, and which graphs attain the maximum. On intersection graphs of paths, the aim is to represent a given graph optimally as the vertex-intersection graph of a collection of paths, where even tight bounds on the order of such a representation remain open. On fractional intersecting families, the lab is working toward a conjectured tight upper bound on the size of a fractional \(\theta\)-intersecting family of subsets of \([n]\).

The lab also has a standing interest in computational and experimental approaches to combinatorial problems, and in mathematical exposition and outreach. Members organize and take part in workshops, reading groups, film screenings, and public talks.
