Research & Directions

My work focuses on extremal problems in graph theory and the structural properties of combinatorial embeddings. Below is a summary of my past and ongoing investigations.

Brualdi–Hoffman-type Extremal Problems IIT Hyderabad | with M. Rajesh Kannan

The spectral version of the classical Turán-type problem, introduced by Nikiforov, asks for bounds on the maximum spectral radius of an $F$-free graph on $n$ vertices. My focus is on the Brualdi–Hoffman-type variant: seeking analogous bounds for $F$-free graphs on $m$ edges.

While the triangle-free case ($F = K_3$) has seen significant attention, our work addresses the next non-trivial case: when $F$ is the complete graph on four vertices ($K_4$).

Intersection Graphs of Paths IIT Bombay | Postdoctoral Work

Every graph can be represented as the vertex-intersection graph of a collection of paths. However, finding optimal path representors—or even establishing tight bounds on their order—remains a difficult problem from both theoretical and computational standpoints.

My work investigates the gap between existence and optimality, exploring how these representations can be constructed for specific graph classes.

Triangulations & Extremal Set Theory IIT Bombay | Advised by Niranjan Balachandran

Graph Coloring & Surfaces: I studied the gap between chromatic and list chromatic numbers for surface-embeddable graphs. This resulted in the complete classification of the colorability of 6-regular triangulations of the torus and a linear-time 5-list-coloring algorithm for a large class of these triangulations.

Fractional Intersecting Systems: In extremal set theory, I investigated fractional variants of $L$-intersecting set systems. This work involved computing the ranks of symmetric, zero-diagonal matrices arising from these families, showing they emerge as generalized adjacency matrices of tournaments and maintain high rank with high probability.
