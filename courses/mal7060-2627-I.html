MAL7060: Statistics for Data Science

Autumn 2026–27 (Semester I) · IIT Jodhpur

Offered for 2024-batch students of the Executive M.Tech. programme in Data and Computational Sciences (DCS) carrying a backlog in this course

Syllabus

Prerequisite: Basic Probability and Statistics. The topics to be covered are as follows:

  1. Probability Models and Sampling Techniques
    • Normal, chi-square, and \(t\) distributions
    • Basic concepts of random sampling, sampling from a normal distribution
    • Properties of the sample mean and sample variance
  2. Estimation
    • Point estimation: maximum likelihood estimator, Bayes estimator
    • Methods of evaluating estimators: unbiasedness, mean squared error
    • Interval estimation: confidence intervals for the mean and the variance of a normal population using the pivot technique
  3. Testing of Hypotheses
    • Basic concepts of statistical hypothesis testing, simple and composite hypotheses, critical regions
    • Type-I and Type-II errors, size and power of a test
    • UMP tests, Neyman–Pearson lemma
    • Tests for one-sample and two-sample problems from normal populations
  4. Association and Correlation Analysis
    • Categorical data, consistency of data, independence and association of attributes
    • Measures of association: Pearson's and Yule's measures, Goodman–Kruskal's \(\gamma\), odds ratio
    • Properties of the product moment correlation coefficient, Spearman's rank correlation coefficient
  5. Mixture Models and Latent Models
    • Basic ideas, finite mixtures, continuous mixtures, conditional mixing distributions
    • Latent models
  6. Stochastic Processes
    • Definition and classification of general stochastic processes
    • Poisson processes: interarrival and waiting time distributions, NHPP, compound Poisson process
    • Renewal theory: distribution of \(N(t)\), Wald's equation, renewal reward processes
    • Random walks
