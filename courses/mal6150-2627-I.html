MAL6150: Probability and Statistics

Autumn 2026–27 (Semester I) · IIT Jodhpur

Syllabus

The primary text for this course is Vijay K. Rohatgi and A. K. M. E. Saleh, An Introduction to Probability and Statistics, 2nd ed., Wiley India, 2000. The topics covered are as follows.

  1. Probability Spaces and Random Variables
    • Probability space, conditional probability, Bayes' theorem
    • Random variable, cumulative distribution function and its properties, probability density function
    • Functions of a random variable, standard discrete and continuous distributions and their applications
    • Transformations, moments, Chebyshev's inequality
  2. Joint Distributions
    • Random vectors, joint, marginal, and conditional distributions
    • Conditional expectation, independence, correlation and regression
    • Bivariate normal distribution, functions of random vectors, transformations
  3. Limit Theorems
    • Convergence of sequences of random variables
    • Weak and strong laws of large numbers, central limit theorem
  4. Estimation and Tests of Hypotheses
    • Sampling distributions, point estimation of parameters
    • Maximum likelihood method and method of moments
    • Interval estimation, testing of hypotheses
